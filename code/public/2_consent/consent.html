What is this study about?

Researchers at Tufts University are conducting a study on different evaluation methods for data visualization tools. Basically, we want to know the best ways to figure out if a data visualization tool is useful. You are being asked to participate because you volunteered. You are one of about 110 participants to take part in this study. Your participation in the study is expected to last 35 minutes

What will happen during this research?

If you agree to be in this research, your participation will include answering a number of survey questions and a number of questions about data visualizations you will see. We will tell you about any new information that may affect your willingness to continue participation in this research.

What will you do to protect my privacy?

The results of the study may be published. We will take measures to protect your privacy and confidentiality including deidentifying your data and storing it in aggregate. None of the data or your performance will be tied to you personally in any way. Your name and any other information that could identify you are not included in Amazon Turk id. The only identifier is your Turk Worker Identification number.

Individuals and organizations responsible for conducting or monitoring this research may be permitted access to and inspect the research records. This includes Tufts Social, Behavioral and Educational Research Institutional Review Board (Tufts SBER IRB). The information collected as part of this research will not be used or distributed for future research studies, even if all of your identifiers are removed.

Identifiers will be removed from any and all identifiable private information. After the removal, the de-identified information could be used for future research studies or distributed to another investigator for future research studies without additional informed consent from you, or a legally authorized representative.

What are the risks or discomforts associated with this research?

The risks or discomforts associated with the research are potentially frustration with the difficulty of data questions. In addition to these, this research may have risks or discomforts that are unknown at this time. If in the future we become aware of any additional risks or discomforts that may affect you, we will tell you.

How might I benefit from this research?

There may be no personal benefit from your participation outside of the Amazon Turk Worker compensation, but the knowledge received may be of value to humanity.

What is the compensation for the research?

You will receive $5.50 for participating in this study.

What will happen if I choose not to participate?

It is your choice to participate or not to participate in this research. Participation is voluntary and there are no consequences to you.

Is my participation voluntary, and can I withdraw?

Taking part in this research study is your decision. Your participation in this study is voluntary. You do not have to take part in this study, but if you do, you can stop at any time. Your decision whether to participate will not affect your relationship with researchers, Tufts University or Amazon Turk. There are no penalty/consequences, or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled. You have the right to choose not to participate in any study activity or completely withdraw from continued participation at any point in this study without penalty/consequences/loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled.

If you withdraw from the study, the data collected to the point of withdrawal will not be considered as part of the analysis and will be deleted.

Can I withdraw from the study?

If you want to withdraw, you can just stop the survey at any time. We will not use your data.
